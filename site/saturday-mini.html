      Why it’s a Saturday

      The grid is fixed. What makes it a Saturday is the clues — and the model can show it. Replaying the solve easiest-first, an entry is a gimme (known cold from the clue), forced (crossings leave one fit), or a hard get (obscure and still open). Raise the clue difficulty and the same 15 open crossings flip from harmless to brutal.

      Fair, not cheap

      Zero open crossings pair two entries below 70 — no roll-your-own Natick. The hardest fill is DORIC (75); the obscurest, IRENE (60), is forced by its crossings, so it never bites.

      How it was picked

      Fills drawn from the crossword list banded to [60, 90] — solid words plus the common glue that makes a dense 5×5 packable at all — then ranked by hard-gets and bottleneck size under Saturday cluing.
