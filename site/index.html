Eleven ways to build a crossword

Every grid below was generated by the engines and clued live by the model behind the clue port — nothing hand-placed. Each one is playable, and each demonstrates a different piece of the system: the dense word-square, black cells found from a count, a 10×10 that fills from a short word list, a black-cell pattern sampled from an energy field, a 9×9 whose entries run past the mini’s five, the difficulty model that can prove a mini is a Saturday, a mini you solve from half its clues because the crossings force the rest, one where only the central cross is left blank, and a flagship — Latent — where the forced solve traces a living thread and ends in a debrief of the structure you conquered. Several come in freeform pairs — the same search with 180° symmetry switched off, so you can see how an asymmetric layout looks and feels next to the mirrored convention.
